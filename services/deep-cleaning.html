Deep House Cleaning Services in the West Valley

The detailed clean. Baseboards, vents, inside appliances, tile and grout, light fixtures — the spots a maintenance clean doesn't cover. Best for first-time clients, seasonal resets, and homes that need to be brought back to baseline.

What deep cleaning means at Spotless

Deep cleaning is the detailed clean. It covers everything a standard residential cleaning covers — floors, surfaces, kitchens, bathrooms — plus the spots that don't get attention on a regular maintenance visit. Baseboards. Vents. The inside of the oven. Behind the toilet. The grout in the shower. Light fixtures. The tracks on sliding doors.

It takes longer because there's more to do. It costs more because it takes longer. And it should be infrequent — most homes need a deep clean once or twice a year, with standard maintenance cleaning in between.

If your home hasn't been professionally cleaned in a while, or if you're a first-time client, we'll usually recommend starting with a deep clean. It resets the baseline. After that, standard cleaning maintains the result at a lower price point.

What's included

A deep clean covers everything in a standard cleaning, plus all of the following:

Throughout the home

  • All baseboards hand-wiped
  • Vents and air registers cleaned
  • Light fixtures dusted and wiped (where reachable)
  • Ceiling fans dusted
  • Door frames and door tops wiped
  • Window sills and tracks cleaned
  • Light switch plates and outlet covers wiped
  • Inside of accessible windows cleaned

Kitchen — deep additions

  • Inside of oven cleaned
  • Inside of microwave deep-cleaned
  • Inside of refrigerator (on request)
  • Range hood and exhaust filter degreased
  • Cabinet exteriors hand-wiped (full surface, not just spot-clean)
  • Backsplash detailed
  • Underneath small appliances cleaned

Bathrooms — deep additions

  • Tile grout scrubbed
  • Shower door tracks detailed
  • Behind and around the toilet base cleaned
  • Exhaust fans dusted
  • Inside cabinet under sink wiped (if accessible)

Bedrooms & living spaces

  • Under furniture vacuumed where movable
  • Headboards and bed frames dusted
  • Closet floors vacuumed
  • Inside of dresser and desk drawers wiped (on request, when empty)

Common situations we handle

Deep cleaning isn't a routine purchase — most clients book one for a specific reason. Some of the most common:

First time hiring a cleaning company

If your home has never been professionally cleaned (or it's been more than a year), a deep clean is the right starting point. It establishes the baseline that recurring cleaning will maintain.

Seasonal reset

Spring and fall in Surprise and across the West Valley bring different cleaning challenges — monsoon dust in late summer, post-holiday buildup in January. A deep clean once or twice a year keeps the home from accumulating problems that compound.

Pre-listing or post-renovation

Selling your home? A deep clean before photos and showings is one of the highest-ROI moves you can make. Same for post-construction or post-renovation cleanup — see our move-in/move-out service for those specific situations.

Allergy or air-quality concerns

Vents, baseboards, ceiling fans, and HVAC registers collect dust and allergens that a maintenance clean doesn't touch. A deep clean every 6-12 months makes a measurable difference for households with allergies.

Hosting a major event

A wedding rehearsal, family reunion, anniversary party — when the entire family is coming through the door, a deep clean the week before takes the home from "fine" to "presented."

Questions about deep cleaning

Frequently asked

How is deep cleaning different from standard cleaning?

Standard cleaning covers floors, surfaces, kitchens, and bathrooms — the visible maintenance clean. Deep cleaning adds detail work: baseboards, vents, inside appliances, grout, light fixtures, behind the toilet. It takes longer and costs more, and most homes only need it once or twice a year.

How long does a deep clean take?

Most deep cleans run 4-8 hours with a two-person crew, depending on home size and condition. Larger homes or homes that haven't been deep-cleaned in years take longer. The instant quote tool factors this in.

Do I need a deep clean before recurring service?

Almost always, yes. New clients with homes that haven't been professionally cleaned recently start with a deep clean to establish baseline, then move to a standard residential cleaning schedule from there.

Can I add specific deep-clean tasks to a regular cleaning?

You can add individual tasks (interior fridge, inside oven, baseboards) à la carte to a standard clean — but if you're adding more than two or three, it's usually more economical to book a deep clean.

Will deep cleaning damage anything?

No. We don't use harsh acids, abrasives, or chemicals that damage finishes. Our crew is trained on which products to use on which surfaces. If you have anything specific that needs special handling — natural stone, hardwood, antique fixtures — flag it when you book.

How often should I deep clean?

Once or twice a year is right for most homes. Households with kids, pets, or allergies often go quarterly. If you're doing standard cleaning monthly or bi-weekly in between, deep cleaning frequency doesn't need to be high.
