House Cleaning in Peoria, AZ

Peoria is home to some of the West Valley's most varied housing stock — from the master-planned communities up north to the older neighborhoods near downtown. We clean throughout the city, in every neighborhood and ZIP code.

Cleaning Peoria from the inside out

Peoria is geographically one of the largest cities in the West Valley, stretching from the Sun City border in the south all the way up to Lake Pleasant in the north. That range means a wide variety of homes — older single-story ranch houses near Old Town, newer two-story builds in Vistancia and Westwing, and everything in between.

We're based a few miles west in Surprise, which puts most of Peoria within an easy 20-minute drive. Our crews work Peoria routes daily, and we've built up familiarity with the access patterns, HOAs, and home types across the city.

Neighborhoods we work in across Peoria

Peoria has more master-planned communities than almost any other West Valley city. Some of the neighborhoods we work in regularly:

  • Vistancia — North Peoria master-planned community with substantial new construction. Larger lots, two-story homes are common, and the community has gated sub-sections.
  • Westwing & Westwing Mountain — Higher-elevation neighborhoods on the city's western edge with views over the valley. Mix of older established homes and newer construction.
  • Trilogy at Vistancia — 55+ active adult community within Vistancia. Smaller home footprints, often used as winter residences for snowbirds.
  • Lake Pleasant area — Homes near the lake and recreation area, including some larger custom builds and second residences.
  • Sonoran Mountain Ranch — Established community with a mix of single-story and two-story homes, mature landscaping.
  • Fletcher Heights — Central Peoria neighborhood with established homes and a strong family-residential character.
  • Old Town Peoria — The historical core of the city with older homes, smaller lots, and a different cleaning rhythm than the master-planned developments.
  • Sun City & surrounding 55+ areas — Where Peoria borders the original Sun City. HOA-governed access, smaller home footprints, often vacant during off-season for snowbirds.

What's particular about cleaning a Peoria home

Cleaning a Peoria home brings the same desert-climate considerations as any West Valley home — dust, hard water, monsoon season — but with a few city-specific patterns we've noticed:

Snowbird scheduling is a real thing

Trilogy and other 55+ communities in north Peoria have substantial snowbird populations. Homes get used heavily October through April, then sit closed up the rest of the year. We handle vacant-home check-in cleans, pre-arrival deep cleans before the homeowner returns, and switch-back to regular schedules when they're in residence. Coordinate this in advance if you're a seasonal resident.

Vistancia's newer construction has a different dust pattern

Newer master-planned communities like Vistancia are still settling — landscaping is maturing, surrounding lots are still being built out. That means more construction-borne dust than older established neighborhoods. Recurring weekly or bi-weekly cleaning makes a noticeable difference here.

Two-story homes need different attention

A lot of Peoria's newer builds are two-story, which means staircases, more square footage to vacuum, and elevated ceiling fans that need real ladder work for proper deep cleaning. Our pricing accounts for two-story homes in the instant quote tool.

Lake Pleasant area homes get different traffic

Homes near Lake Pleasant — particularly second residences used for weekends — have a different wear pattern. They sit empty during the week, then get heavy use Friday through Sunday. Bi-weekly Sunday-evening or Monday-morning cleans are the most common cadence we run there.
